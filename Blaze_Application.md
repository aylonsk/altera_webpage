9. Please describe your venture idea (2-4 sentences)

Altera Labs is an institution-level learning-verification and knowledge-tracking infrastructure delivered through a state-aware AI tutoring interface. As educational institutions face a pedagogical crisis of cognitive offloading, over 50% of students now rely on AI to generate answers and bypass problem-solving altogether (arXiv:2412.02653). Our platform directly addresses this challenge by replacing stateless answer machines with an Adaptive Knowledge Tracking engine that actively guides students through the reasoning process. By dynamically modeling each student's learning progression and equipping educators with real-time insight into concept mastery, Altera Labs empowers universities to adopt AI in ways that strengthen learning outcomes rather than replace critical thinking.

12. Further describe the problem your idea/venture addresses in under three sentences:

A February 2025 HEPI/Kortext study showed that 88% of undergraduate students now use generative AI, a sharp increase from 53% in 2024. As these tools become more common, many students rely on them to generate answers rather than work through problems themselves, weakening critical thinking and making it harder for instructors to observe students’ reasoning processes and identify where misconceptions form.
As AI continues to reshape learning behaviors, universities increasingly need tools that help them monitor learning progress at scale, identify where students struggle, and enable instructors to intervene earlier.

13. Describe your venture’s approach to addressing this problem/opportunity. How is the approach unique? (200-word limit)

Our solution provides institutions with a way to encourage responsible learning with AI. Students increasingly rely on Generative AI for answers without understanding the reasoning behind them, directly undermining learning. Altera Labs addresses this by posing guiding questions, hints, and structured steps. Rather than replacing the learning with answers, our adaptive engine monitors and responds to a student's evolving knowledge state. Instructors, in turn, gain real-time insight with learning analytics dashboards that display gaps in students' learning, allowing them to identify misconceptions and intervene earlier. 
Additionally, our technical approach reflects our market strategy in targeting institutions. Through Fuel, we identified our market opportunity as educational institutions because they need personalized infrastructure to ensure students graduate with skills to excel. We identified that their solution is not just a chatbot, but it is a fully-integrated learning platform that leverages AI to continuously track student progress. Many AI study tools are optimized for individual use and instant answers, while our system is designed to integrate into instructional workflows and provide instructors with actionable learning insights. By embedding Altera directly into schools and organizations, we create compounding impact and steadier revenue than any individual-first model can sustain.

14. What progress have you already achieved on this project? (200-word limit)

We built an early beta and recruited 49 student users. Their engagement proved our core assumption: if you give students a Socratic AI instead of a tool that just spits out the answer, they will actually use it to learn. Winning the Audience Award at the JHU FUEL Demo Day backed this up, bringing us a steady pipeline of interested users and validating that students really want this.

But getting students to log in is only step one. The real hurdle is proving they actually learn better. Our next major milestone—which this Blaze grant directly funds—is a 50-student Fall 2026 pilot in a JHU Calculus course. This pilot moves us past basic product testing and into formal efficacy validation. By matching the 'knowledge maps' generated by our Bayesian Knowledge Tracing engine against real student homework and exam scores, we will have mathematical proof that our software improves grades.

At the same time, we're actively sitting down with university IT admins and department heads to understand their purchasing cycles. Having that hard pedagogical data is the missing key; it's what transforms us from a cool student project into a necessary tool that Deans will actually pay $30/student/course for, unlocking our $127,200 Year 1 revenue projection.

16. How do you anticipate generating revenue? Please explain your business model to the best of your ability. (200-word limit)

Altera Labs generates recurring revenue through a B2B institutional licensing model, charging a $30 per student, per course license fee—a structure validating user willingness to pay by aligning with established instructional SaaS platforms like Packback and Gradescope. Our Total Addressable Market (TAM) is $5.5 billion, based on the 18.4 million higher-education students in the US taking 10 courses annually. We are targeting a Serviceable Available Market (SAM) of 3.3 million STEM students at AI-ready institutions, an accessible $100 million opportunity.

Our go-to-market strategy targets a Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) of 24,000 students across early-adopter institutions, equating to $0.7 million in initial market capture. We execute via a 'land and expand' strategy: initiating semester-long departmental pilots to generate the indisputable BKT efficacy data administrators require. 

Upon demonstrating clear ROI, we convert these pilots into paid annual contracts at the departmental or university level. This model de-risks initial adoption while embedding our analytics deeply into their core academic workflows. By providing continuous visibility into institutional academic integrity and conceptual mastery, we create high switching costs. This strategic lock-in sustains long-term recurring revenue, driving our financial models from $127,200 in Year 1 revenue to over $1 million by Year 3.
